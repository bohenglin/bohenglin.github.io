About Me

Hi there, thank you for visiting my page. My name is Boheng Lin (林柏亨, pronouns: he, him, his). Here are some facts about myself:

I was born in Sichuan, China, the province in southwest China with giant pandas and hot pots. I was easily sick in my early childhood, so my parents sent me to learn martial arts when I was about four years old. The greatest achievement then was being a finalist in a provincial martial arts competition when I was about six - I guess, the exact year has long gone from my memory. Under the influence by both of my parents, I too take education seriously and embrace every opportunity available. My family moved from a small city in Sichuan, to the capital of Sichuan when I was 11 years old, then moved to Singapore half way through my secondary school and high school (or junior college). After earning a bachelor's degree in University of Massachusetts Amherst, I am currenlty pursuing a master's degree at Tufts University in the United States.

During the process, I have challenged many things and myself have changed in many ways. For example, in my first year at Bedok View Secondary School in Singapore - also the first year I flew abroad myself - I joined the National Cadet Corps (NCC) as a private. This is an extra curriculum activities, or the equivalent of ROTC in Singapore. As an international student, I worked from private, to assistant sergeant major leading the more than fifty cadets. I also earned the qualifications as a Freestyle Drill Instructor. In high school, I (accidentally) joined the competitive swim team without prior swim experience. Yet I became the boy's team captain after one year of training, and competed in the 57th nationals as a finalist - it was indeed difficult to train alongside with other professional swimmers, but I enjoyed it somehow (see how obvious my goggle tan was below).

The experience of staying in different environements, studying, living and working with people from different backgrounds has changed me to be more open, and accepting. I am more intrigued to explore, to try, and to discover things that I have not done before or experienced before. After all, life is a process.

Having said that, I am thankful for my parents, for it is them who allowed all these wonderful things happen to me, and I am looking forward to bring good results to them in the end.
